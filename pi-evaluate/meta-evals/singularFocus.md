Does the criterion evaluate one quality dimension rather than multiple dimensions that can vary independently?

A quality dimension is the property being graded on the criterion's scoring scale. Two dimensions vary independently when changing one does not determine the other: a response can rank higher on dimension A while ranking lower on dimension B, and another response can rank lower on A while ranking higher on B.

Score 0 - The criterion combines two or more independently varying dimensions that can point to different scores for the same response. Example: "accurate and engaging" combines factual correctness with writing style; an accurate but dull response and an inaccurate but engaging response would be scored differently depending on which dimension is weighted more.

Score 1 - The criterion combines dimensions that share a topic, but those dimensions can still point to different scores unless the rubric states how to weight them. Example: "complete and concise" combines coverage with length; adding detail can increase coverage while lowering concision, and removing detail can increase concision while lowering coverage.

Score 2 - The criterion grades one dimension. Any sub-properties named in the criterion are evidence for that same dimension rather than separate dimensions that can point to different scores. Example: "coherence" may use logical order and consistency as evidence because disorder and contradiction both lower the same coherence score.
