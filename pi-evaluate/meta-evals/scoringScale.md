Does the criterion specify a discrete scoring scale whose levels have non-overlapping conditions?

Score 0 - No scoring scale is stated, or the scale is continuous/open-ended rather than discrete (for example 0-1 decimals, 1-100 points, percentages, or "rate from 1 to 10").

Score 1 - A discrete scoring scale is stated, but at least one score level lacks stated conditions, examples, or boundaries that distinguish it from adjacent levels.

Score 2 - The scale is binary {0,1} or triple {0,1,2}, and every score level states the evidence, condition, or boundary that assigns that score rather than another score.
